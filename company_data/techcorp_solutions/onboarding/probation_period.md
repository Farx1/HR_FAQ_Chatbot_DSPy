# TechCorp Solutions - Probationary Period Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** January 1, 2024  
**Policy Number:** HR-ONB-002

## 1. Overview

All new employees at TechCorp Solutions undergo a 90-day probationary period. This period allows both the employee and the company to assess mutual fit and ensure alignment with role expectations.

## 2. Duration

### 2.1 Standard Probation

- **Length:** 90 calendar days from start date
- **Applies to:** All new hires regardless of level
- **Start date:** First day of employment

### 2.2 Extensions

Probation may be extended by up to 30 days if:
- Performance is borderline
- Insufficient time to assess due to circumstances
- Additional training or support is needed

Extensions require:
- HR approval
- Written notification to employee
- Clear expectations for extended period

## 3. Expectations During Probation

### 3.1 Employee Expectations

- Complete all onboarding requirements
- Attend required training sessions
- Meet performance milestones
- Demonstrate alignment with company values
- Maintain regular attendance
- Communicate proactively with manager

### 3.2 Manager Expectations

- Provide clear role expectations
- Conduct weekly 1:1 meetings
- Offer regular feedback
- Document performance observations
- Provide necessary resources and support
- Complete 30, 60, and 90-day reviews

## 4. Review Process

### 4.1 Check-in Schedule

| Timing | Type | Purpose |
|--------|------|---------|
| Week 1 | Daily check-ins | Immediate questions and support |
| Week 2-4 | Weekly 1:1s | Progress tracking |
| Day 30 | Formal review | First milestone assessment |
| Day 31-60 | Weekly 1:1s | Continued development |
| Day 60 | Formal review | Second milestone assessment |
| Day 61-90 | Weekly 1:1s | Final preparation |
| Day 90 | Final review | Probation decision |

### 4.2 30-Day Review

Focus areas:
- Completion of onboarding tasks
- Understanding of role and responsibilities
- Initial performance observations
- Cultural fit indicators
- Questions and concerns

Outcome: Written feedback with any areas for improvement.

### 4.3 60-Day Review

Focus areas:
- Progress on identified improvement areas
- Contribution to team projects
- Relationship building
- Skill development
- Alignment with expectations

Outcome: Written assessment and trajectory discussion.

### 4.4 90-Day Final Review

Focus areas:
- Overall performance assessment
- Achievement of probation goals
- Cultural alignment
- Future potential
- Development plan

Outcome: Probation completion or termination decision.

## 5. Performance Standards

### 5.1 Assessment Criteria

| Category | Weight | Indicators |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Job Performance | 40% | Quality of work, productivity, skill application |
| Learning & Adaptation | 25% | Speed of learning, applying feedback, growth |
| Collaboration | 20% | Teamwork, communication, relationship building |
| Cultural Fit | 15% | Values alignment, attitude, professionalism |

### 5.2 Rating Scale

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Exceeds | Performing above expectations for new hire |
| Meets | On track, meeting expected milestones |
| Developing | Needs improvement in some areas |
| Does Not Meet | Significant gaps in performance |

## 6. Benefits During Probation

### 6.1 Available Benefits

All benefits are available from Day 1:
- Health, dental, vision insurance
- 401(k) participation (match begins after 1 year)
- Paid time off (accrues from Day 1)
- Sick leave
- Company holidays

### 6.2 Limitations

During probation:
- Internal transfers not permitted
- Remote work may be limited (manager discretion)
- Tuition reimbursement not available

## 7. Time Off During Probation

### 7.1 Guidelines

- PTO accrues from Day 1
- Taking PTO during probation is discouraged but not prohibited
- Excessive absences may impact probation assessment
- Pre-planned time off (disclosed before hire) is honored

### 7.2 Sick Leave

- Sick leave available as needed
- Extended illness may result in probation extension
- Documentation required for absences over 3 days

## 8. Probation Outcomes

### 8.1 Successful Completion

If probation is successful:
- Written confirmation provided
- Full employee status confirmed
- Eligible for internal opportunities
- All benefits fully active
- Performance goals set for next quarter

### 8.2 Probation Extension

If extension is needed:
- Maximum 30-day extension
- Written notice with specific expectations
- Increased check-in frequency
- Clear success criteria defined

### 8.3 Termination

If probation is unsuccessful:
- Employment ends at or before 90 days
- Final paycheck includes accrued PTO
- COBRA information provided
- Exit interview conducted
- Equipment returned

## 9. Employee Rights

### 9.1 During Probation

- Receive regular feedback
- Ask questions and seek clarification
- Request additional support or training
- Raise concerns without retaliation
- Access HR for guidance

### 9.2 Termination Appeal

- Probationary employees may request meeting with HR
- Concerns documented and reviewed
- No formal appeal process during probation
- At-will employment applies

## 10. Manager Responsibilities

### 10.1 Documentation

Managers must document:
- All formal reviews (30, 60, 90 days)
- Performance concerns as they arise
- Feedback provided and employee response
- Support and resources offered

### 10.2 Decision Making

Before recommending termination:
- Consult with HR Business Partner
- Review documentation
- Consider if adequate support was provided
- Ensure decision is objective and fair

## 11. Remote Employee Probation

### 11.1 Additional Considerations

For remote new hires:
- More frequent video check-ins
- Virtual onboarding buddy assigned
- Clear communication expectations
- On-site visit during probation (if possible)

### 11.2 Assessment

Remote performance assessed on:
- Communication responsiveness
- Meeting participation
- Work output quality
- Self-management capability
