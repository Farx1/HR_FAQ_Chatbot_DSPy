# TechCorp Solutions - Anti-Harassment & Discrimination Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** January 1, 2024  
**Policy Number:** HR-POL-004

## 1. Policy Statement

TechCorp Solutions is committed to providing a work environment free from harassment, discrimination, and retaliation. We have zero tolerance for any form of harassment or discrimination based on protected characteristics.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Contractors and consultants
- Interns and volunteers
- Vendors and visitors
- All work-related settings (office, remote, events, travel)

## 3. Protected Characteristics

Harassment and discrimination based on the following are prohibited:

- Race, color, ethnicity
- National origin, ancestry
- Religion, creed
- Sex, gender, gender identity, gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age (40 and over)
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Genetic information
- Marital status
- Pregnancy, childbirth, related conditions
- Veteran or military status
- Any other characteristic protected by law

## 4. Definitions

### 4.1 Harassment

Unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that:
- Creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive work environment
- Unreasonably interferes with work performance
- Affects employment decisions

**Examples include:**
- Offensive jokes, slurs, epithets
- Physical threats or intimidation
- Displaying offensive images or objects
- Unwanted physical contact
- Cyberbullying or online harassment

### 4.2 Sexual Harassment

Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, or other verbal/physical conduct of a sexual nature when:
- Submission is a condition of employment
- Submission affects employment decisions
- Conduct interferes with work or creates a hostile environment

**Examples include:**
- Unwanted touching, hugging, kissing
- Sexual comments, jokes, or innuendos
- Displaying sexually explicit materials
- Repeated unwanted requests for dates
- Sexual gestures or leering
- Sending sexual messages or images

### 4.3 Discrimination

Treating someone unfavorably because of a protected characteristic in:
- Hiring and firing
- Compensation and benefits
- Promotions and assignments
- Training and development
- Any other term or condition of employment

## 5. Reporting Procedures

### 5.1 How to Report

If you experience or witness harassment or discrimination:

1. **Direct Communication** (if comfortable): Tell the person their behavior is unwelcome
2. **Report to Manager**: Speak with your direct manager
3. **Report to HR**: Contact HR directly
4. **Anonymous Hotline**: Call 1-800-555-SAFE (7233)
5. **Online Report**: hr.techcorp-solutions.com/report

### 5.2 Information to Provide

- Names of individuals involved
- Date(s), time(s), and location(s)
- Description of the incident(s)
- Names of witnesses (if any)
- Any documentation (emails, messages, photos)

### 5.3 Manager Responsibilities

Managers who receive complaints or observe harassment must:
- Take the report seriously
- Report to HR within 24 hours
- Maintain confidentiality
- Not retaliate or allow retaliation
- Support the affected employee

## 6. Investigation Process

### 6.1 Investigation Steps

1. **Acknowledgment**: HR acknowledges report within 24 hours
2. **Initial Assessment**: Determine scope and urgency
3. **Investigation**: Interview parties and witnesses, review evidence
4. **Findings**: Document conclusions
5. **Resolution**: Determine appropriate action
6. **Follow-up**: Check in with reporting party

### 6.2 Timeline

- Investigations typically completed within **30 days**
- Complex cases may take longer
- Updates provided to reporting party throughout

### 6.3 Confidentiality

- Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- All parties expected to maintain confidentiality
- Breach of confidentiality may result in discipline

## 7. Consequences

Violations of this policy may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Required training
- Suspension (with or without pay)
- Demotion
- Termination of employment
- Legal action

Severity of consequences depends on:
- Nature and severity of conduct
- Whether conduct was repeated
- Employee's disciplinary history

## 8. Retaliation Prohibited

Retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports harassment or discrimination
- Participates in an investigation
- Opposes discriminatory practices

**Is strictly prohibited and will result in discipline up to termination.**

Examples of retaliation:
- Demotion or termination
- Negative performance reviews
- Exclusion from meetings or projects
- Hostile treatment by colleagues

## 9. Prevention and Training

### 9.1 Mandatory Training

- All employees: Annual harassment prevention training
- Managers: Additional training on handling complaints
- New hires: Training within first 30 days

### 9.2 Prevention Measures

- Clear communication of expectations
- Regular policy reminders
- Open-door policy for concerns
- Anonymous feedback channels
