# TechCorp Solutions - 401(k) Retirement Plan

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** January 1, 2024  
**Policy Number:** HR-BEN-002

## 1. Overview

TechCorp Solutions offers a competitive 401(k) retirement savings plan to help employees build financial security for the future. Our plan includes generous employer matching and a variety of investment options.

## 2. Eligibility

### 2.1 Enrollment Eligibility

- All employees age 21 or older
- Full-time and part-time employees
- Eligible after **90 days** of employment
- Automatic enrollment at 3% contribution (opt-out available)

### 2.2 Employer Match Eligibility

- Must complete **1 year** of service for employer match
- Must work at least **1,000 hours** in the plan year

## 3. Contribution Limits

### 3.1 Employee Contributions (2024)

| Age | Annual Limit |
|-----|--------------|
| Under 50 | $23,000 |
| 50 and older | $30,500 (includes $7,500 catch-up) |

### 3.2 Contribution Types

**Traditional 401(k):**
- Pre-tax contributions
- Reduces current taxable income
- Taxes paid upon withdrawal in retirement

**Roth 401(k):**
- After-tax contributions
- No current tax benefit
- Tax-free withdrawals in retirement (if qualified)

## 4. Employer Match

### 4.1 Match Formula

TechCorp Solutions matches **100% of the first 6%** of your salary that you contribute.

**Example:**
- Salary: $100,000
- Employee contributes: 6% ($6,000)
- Employer match: 100% of 6% ($6,000)
- Total annual contribution: $12,000

### 4.2 Match Vesting Schedule

| Years of Service | Vesting Percentage |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Less than 1 year | 0% |
| 1 year | 25% |
| 2 years | 50% |
| 3 years | 75% |
| 4+ years | 100% |

**Note:** Your own contributions are always 100% vested.

### 4.3 True-Up Contribution

- TechCorp provides a year-end "true-up" contribution
- Ensures you receive full match even if contributions vary throughout the year

## 5. Investment Options

### 5.1 Available Funds

| Category | Options |
|----------|---------|
| Target Date Funds | Vanguard Target Retirement 2025-2065 |
| Stock Index | Vanguard 500 Index, Total Stock Market Index |
| International | Vanguard International Growth, Emerging Markets |
| Bond | Vanguard Total Bond Market, TIPS |
| Stable Value | Fidelity Stable Value Fund |
| Company Stock | TechCorp Solutions Stock (max 10% of portfolio) |

### 5.2 Default Investment

If no selection made, contributions go to:
- Target Date Fund based on expected retirement age (age 65)

### 5.3 Investment Changes

- Change investments anytime through plan portal
- No fees for fund changes
- Changes effective next business day

## 6. Loans

### 6.1 Loan Availability

- Borrow up to **50%** of vested balance
- Maximum loan: **$50,000**
- Minimum loan: **$1,000**

### 6.2 Loan Terms

- General purpose loan: Up to 5 years
- Home purchase loan: Up to 15 years
- Interest rate: Prime rate + 1%
- Origination fee: $75

### 6.3 Repayment

- Automatic payroll deduction
- Missed payments may result in loan default (taxable distribution)
- Loans must be repaid within 60 days if employment ends

## 7. Withdrawals

### 7.1 In-Service Withdrawals

| Type | Requirements |
|------|--------------|
| Hardship | Documented financial hardship |
| Age 59½ | Available without penalty |
| After-tax contributions | Available anytime |

### 7.2 Hardship Withdrawals

Permitted for:
- Medical expenses
- Purchase of primary residence
- Tuition and education fees
- Prevention of eviction
- Funeral expenses
- Home repair from casualty

**Note:** Hardship withdrawals subject to 10% early withdrawal penalty if under 59½.

### 7.3 Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

- Must begin by April 1 following the year you turn 73
- Or upon retirement, if later (unless 5% owner)

## 8. Rollovers

### 8.1 Rolling Into TechCorp 401(k)

You may roll over funds from:
- Previous employer 401(k) plans
- Traditional IRAs
- 403(b) plans

### 8.2 Rolling Out of TechCorp 401(k)

Upon separation, you may:
- Leave funds in plan (if balance > $5,000)
- Roll over to new employer plan
- Roll over to IRA
- Take cash distribution (taxes and penalties may apply)
